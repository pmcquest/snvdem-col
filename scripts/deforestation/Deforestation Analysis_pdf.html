Subnational democracy and forest loss in Colombia

Based on the 2026 deforestation handoff memo. This deck focuses on the data, validation, and the institutional interpretation of the findings.

Prepared for: SN V-Dem team meeting
Location: scripts/deforestation

Research question

  • Can subnational democracy explain deforestation patterns in Colombia?
  • Does MapBiomas capture institutionally-relevant loss better than Hansen GFC?
  • Does snelect show a non-linear frontier relationship?

Data & validation

  • Municipality-year panel for Colombia, 2001–2023.
  • Democracy measure: snelect (elections component).
  • Forest loss sources: Hansen GFC and MapBiomas.
  • Cross-source validation: Pearson r = 0.77, Spearman ρ = 0.63.

Main finding

  • Non-linear U-shaped relationship.
  • Very low snelect: low loss (remote, weak frontier pressure).
  • Mid snelect: peak loss (frontier zones, weak institutions).
  • High snelect: lower loss (stronger institutional capacity).

Robustness and mechanism

  • MapBiomas captures land-use transitions, not all canopy disturbance.
  • This makes it more relevant for institutional impact.
  • Stronger correlation would support the democracy → land-use mechanism.

Temporal evidence

  • Post-2016 Peace Accord surge is visible in the series.
  • Former conflict zones opened to new clearing pressure.
  • Subnational variation is essential to see this effect.

Global and conceptual fit

  • Colombia is a hard test: functioning state, conservation law, conflict transition.
  • National scores would miss within-country variation entirely.
  • GFC and SN-VDem are globally available, so the pipeline is portable.

Recommendation

  • Use this analysis as a proof of concept for subnational democracy research.
  • Frame MapBiomas as the institutionally relevant loss measure.
  • Highlight the U-shaped relationship and post-2016 forest loss surge.
