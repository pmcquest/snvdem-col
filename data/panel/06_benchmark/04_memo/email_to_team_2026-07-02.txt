Following up on today's meeting — here's where the benchmarking work stands, with both memos attached.

WHERE THIS PICKS UP
--------------------
Memo 1 (June 26, attached) laid out the problem: our two dimensions — elections (EL_col_mt,
V-Dem's latent scale) and civil liberties (CL_col_mt, [0,1] scale) — couldn't be combined into
a single sndem_final without distorting the trend. Every method we tried produced an inverted,
flattened, or exaggerated result.

Michael's reply resolved the open questions: benchmarked scores are the intended final measure
for all uses, and the two dimensions should be combined by converting civil liberties to a
Z-score with qnorm(). Memo 2 (attached) implements that and lays out where we've landed.

WHAT WE HAVE NOW
-----------------
Two validated measures of Colombian subnational democracy, built for different purposes:

- Unbenchmarked (sndem) — each municipality's position relative to other Colombian
  municipalities. Best for within-Colombia analysis: what predicts relative democratic
  quality across municipalities. This is the version behind the original spatial-variation
  maps, and it's the paper's core empirical contribution — V-Dem's national-level estimates
  can't show this.

- Benchmarked (sndem_final) — each municipality's position relative to the full V-Dem
  country-year distribution (2000-2023, all countries), anchored directly to Colombia's real
  national trajectory. Best whenever we want an absolute reference point: Colombia's
  trajectory against its own V-Dem history, and — once we extend the pipeline — direct
  comparison to other countries' benchmarked scores.

Memo 2 has the full walkthrough: how each is calculated (with the actual formulas), what each
tells us, validation against the raw V-Dem national estimates, and some suggested language for
distinguishing the two in the paper.
