WHAT I WAS TRYING TO DO
-----------------------
The benchmark script produces two dimension-level outputs anchored to V-Dem's national
subnational estimates: EL_col_mt (elections, on the V-Dem latent scale ~[-3.5, 3.5])
and CL_col_mt (civil liberties, on [0,1] anchored to v2x_civlib). I was trying to
combine these into a single sndem_benchmark on a common scale. Every approach I tried
— within-year ecdf, within-bounds normalization, pnorm transformation, cross-panel
linear rescaling — produced either an inverted trend, a flattened trend, or an
exaggerated trend that didn't match what the individual dimensions show.


WHAT I THINK THE UNDERLYING ISSUE IS
-------------------------------------
The geopredictors are CDF-standardized over the full 2000–2023 panel, which captures
cross-sectional variation in democratic quality across municipalities but does not track
absolute improvement over time. The V-Dem anchors (v2elffelr, CLSNmean) do track
absolute improvement. Any normalization that tries to merge these two types of
information either removes the temporal signal or distorts its shape. This isn't a bug
in the code — it's a structural tension between how the geopredictors and the V-Dem
anchors represent democratic quality.


A CLOSE READING OF THE OPERATIONAL STRATEGY THAT RAISED A QUESTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Re-reading the revised operational strategy document, I noticed a tension between two
statements:

The document says: "We conceive of free and fair elections at the municipal level as a
set of deviations centered around this summary subnational measure [v2elffelr]." This
reads like a theoretical commitment — the benchmarked score (EL_col_mt) is what the
measure is, not just a calibration step. If so, the benchmark would be required even for
within-country mapping and regression, not only for cross-national comparison.

But the document also says the calibration formula is "to complete the calibration of
the data for cross-national comparisons" — which implies benchmarking is supplementary
and within-country analysis can proceed with the unbenchmarked sndem.

These two statements point in different directions, and the answer determines which
variable I should be using for the maps and regression analyses in the Colombia
application.


THREE QUESTIONS I'M NOT SURE I CAN ANSWER WITHOUT YOUR INPUT
-------------------------------------------------------------
1. Is "we conceive of free and fair elections as deviations centered around v2elffelr"
   a theoretical commitment that makes the benchmarked scores the intended final output
   for all uses — or is it describing the motivating logic for a calibration step whose
   outputs are only needed for cross-national comparison?

   This determines whether prior descriptive maps and regressions using sndem are using
   the correct variable.

2. If the benchmarked scores are the intended final measure, is there a principled way
   to combine EL_col_mt (latent scale) and CL_col_mt ([0,1] scale) into a single
   sndem_benchmark? The methodology documents show these as separate figures and don't
   describe a combination rule. This is the main implementation bottleneck.

3. Is it the intent of the CDF standardization that sndem captures relative democratic
   quality at each point in time (cross-sectional validity), with temporal trends being
   secondary? Or should a different standardization approach be used to better preserve
   absolute temporal information?
