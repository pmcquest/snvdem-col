## What changed this afternoon

### 1. Real bug found and fixed: inverted category weights in `weighted_range`/`wtdCL_range`

Prompted by Patrick noticing the civil-liberties range plot looked narrower than the example in
`06_benchmark/Revised operational strategy_Jan2026.docx`. Checked the actual V-Dem codebook for
`v2elsnlsff`/`v2clrgunev`: the response scale runs opposite to what you'd guess (`0` =
"significantly different" should get weight 2, `2` = "no variation" should get weight 0). The
formulas had this backwards, which systematically *understated* both ranges (verified against
real coder-level data for Colombia — coders pick "significant variation" far more often than "no
variation" in most years). Fixed in `04_vdem_data/02_vdem_weighting/02_vdem_weighting.R`; full
writeup in `04_vdem_data/weighting_bug_log_2026-07-03.md`. Also fixed a secondary, unrelated path
bug found while fixing this (`out_dir` pointed at a `coder-level/MC/` directory that never
existed on disk — `write_dta()` was failing outright).

Magnitude (Colombia, 2000-2023 means): `weighted_range` 0.541 → 0.810, `wtdCL_range` 0.036 →
0.069. Final `sndem_final` shifted by up to 0.34 (global-SD units) for some municipality-years;
old/new correlation 0.981 (rankings mostly preserved, individual values shift meaningfully).

### 2. Missing-predictor ("na.rm") decision resolved in `05_weighting`

The open decision flagged in `README.md` since 2026-07-02. Traced all 682 NAs in
`snelect`/`sncivlib` to the predictor side only (`03_geocoded_panel`'s `CDF_averages.rds`), 60
municipalities, mostly small/remote departments. Investigated the na.rm + renormalize tradeoff in
detail (all 682 rescuable in principle, median 88% weight mass retained, but a ~44-row tail
retains as little as 18%). Patrick decided: na.rm + renormalize with a 50% weight-mass floor.
Implemented as `weighted_avg_narm()` in `05_weighting/01_weighting_geopredictors/01_weighting_geopredictors.R`.
NAs dropped 682 → 48. Full writeup in `05_weighting/narm_decision_log_2026-07-03.md`.

**Notable side-finding, not yet investigated:** the 48 remaining NAs are entirely Santa Rosalía
(`99624`) and Cumaribo (`99773`) — the *same two municipalities* flagged for legacy DIVIPOLA
codes in this morning's empirical-merge fix. Missing 12 of 16 predictors in *every* year
suggests a residual `03_geocoded_panel` geocoding/spatial-join gap tied to those renamed codes,
separate from today's na.rm decision. Worth a dedicated look.

### 3. Elections-vs-civil-liberties scale mismatch, explained and cited

Patrick asked why elections lands on a z-score-like scale while civil liberties is bounded
[0,1], and whether the CL weights could be reformulated to avoid `qnorm()`. Answer, now cited
directly from `04_vdem_data/01_source_files/V-Dem/methodology.pdf` (V-Dem Methodology v15,
Coppedge et al., March 2025, sec 2.3, p.6): `v2elffelr` is a single-item "component" (C)
variable straight from the IRT measurement model (unbounded latent scale); `v2x_civlib` is built
through Bayesian factor analysis, and V-Dem's own methodology explicitly converts BFA composites
to [0,1] "using the cumulative distribution function of the normal distribution." So the scale
mismatch is baked in upstream by V-Dem's own construction, not fixable by reformulating our
weights — `qnorm()` in `01_benchmark.R` is the mathematically correct inverse, not an
approximation. Comment added to `02_vdem_weighting.R`; paragraph inserted into
`06_benchmark/memo/benchmarking_memo2_2026-07-02.md` (Section 2, after the anchor table).
