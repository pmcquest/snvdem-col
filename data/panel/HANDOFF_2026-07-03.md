### 2. Real bug found and fixed: 24x row-duplication in the empirical merge

The two candidate merge scripts in `01_empirical_data/04_merge_empirical/`
(`z1_merge_all_raw.R`, `z1_merge_all_raw2.R`) were reviewed, and neither was made canonical
as-is. Instead, `01_merge_empirical.R` was written new, taking the better parts of each (see
that script's inline comments and `README.md` Step 1 section for the full writeup). While
verifying it, found that `df03` (geographic position, criteria 5-9) had been assumed static
by both prior scripts, but actually carries 24 identical yearly rows per municipality
upstream. Joining it as static caused a 24x row explosion (700,128 rows instead of 29,172),
which didn't fail until 3 stages downstream in the imputation-merge step (`01_merge_imputed.R`,
an 8GB "cannot allocate vector" error). Root-caused by direct data inspection, fixed by
joining `df03` on `(MPIO_CDPMP, year)` like the other panel-varying datasets.

Also fixed while merging: two legacy/duplicate DIVIPOLA codes (`23685`→`23580` Puerto
Libertador; `99572`→`99624` Santa Rosalía; `99760`→`99773` Cumaribo) that `z1_merge_all_raw.R`
had been recoding *backwards* (current codes onto legacy ones), which would have silently
dropped those two municipalities from every downstream join.

### 3. Three environment gaps in imputation scripts (not data bugs)

- `imp1214_PopGBIv2.R` and `imp1011_CrimeMI-PMM_v3.R` call `str_pad()` without loading
  `stringr` — added the missing `library()` call to both.
- `imp1011_CrimeMI-PMM_v3.R` needs `mice`, `imp1011_FAviolence_pmq.R` needs `Hmisc` — neither
  installed in this R environment; installed via `install.packages()`.

None of these affected imputation logic (each script's `write_rds()` happens before the point
of failure), but they stopped the scripts from completing cleanly on a fresh run.

### 4. Full pipeline re-run and verified, source-to-final

duplicate `(MPIO_CDPMP, year)` keys, 682 NAs in `snelect`/`sncivlib` (expected, from the
`na.last = "keep"` CDF decision — see README Step 2).
